The Perfect Fish Recipe

Description

A very simple recipe for cooking the perfect fish.

ingredients

  • Any whole fish you want to cook
  • Butter (raw and not processed if you can).
  • Salt to taste though not necessary if you're using salted butter.

For Roasting

  1. Pre-heat an oven to 180Â°C
  2. Prepare your fish i.e: Scale, Gut and De-fin if you wish, your fish.
  3. Place in an oven proof stainless steel dish.
  4. Pop in the oven for anywhere from 30 to 50 minutes - see notes at the bottom of the page.
  5. Once the fish skin has crisped and turned golden, remove from the oven.
  6. Place as much butter as you want in an oven proof dish like a ramekin, allow it to melt but not burn. This won't take long.
  7. Pore half the butter oven the fish, then serve the fish and pore the remaining butter over the fish evenly and enjoy.
  8. Don't forget to salt if your butter was unsalted.

For Grilling on Fire

  1. Prepare your fish i.e: Scale, Gut and De-fin if you wish, your fish.
  2. Place your fish into a fish grilling basket.
  3. Place over the fire and grill for 10-20 minutes.
  4. Flip the basket once the first side has turned golden crispy and cook for another 10 to 20 minutes, see notes for time.
  5. Once the second side has turned golden crispy, remove from the fire and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  6. While the fish rests, melt the butter in a ramekin over the fire, or in an oven - please don't use a microwave.
  7. Serve the fish, pore the butter over it and enjoy.
  8. Again, salt to taste if you want and/or if you used unsalted butter

Additional Notes on Cooking Times

THe cooking time of your fish will vary depending on your size of fish.

Whether you're grilling it on a fire or roasting it in the oven and the temperature you use.

If you've filleted your fish and only cooking fillets, well that will be almost instant and that should be done in a hot grill pan for best results.

Really big, thick and fat fish will take 50 minutes in the oven, whereas smaller more narrow fish will obviously cook in much less time - use your intuition here.

You can also cook it on the open exposed coal if you have nothing to cook it in or on, in this case, don't waste your time scaling the fish and just pop it on the fire, flip when you think you need to and remove. Peal the skin with the scales off and eat the fish off the bone. Very nice.
